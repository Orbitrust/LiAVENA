SISIG

ㅤㅤㅤ

Sisig is a savory and fatty Filipino dish that originated in Pampanga. It is usually made from the chopped-up parts of a pig's face and belly, seasoned with vinegar, calamansi, onions, chili peppers, etc. The prototype of the modern sisig came from the period of the American Occupation, where unused pig heads from the Clark Air Base in Pampanga would be bought by residents to turn into sisig. Lucia Cunanan then allowed the sisig to truly expand across the country by expounding on its recipe, making it a national dish known by everyone. Sisig can be found all over the world today, revolutionizing Filipino culture as well as reflecting our heritage through it.


INGREDIENTS RECIPE
  • 1 kg pork face, ears, cheeks, etc.ㅤㅤㅤㅤ

  • 250 g pork belly

  • 1 pc onion, chopped

  • 5 cups water

  • ½ cup butter

  • 3 pcs calamansi, or 1 pc lemon

  • 3 tbsp soy sauce

  • 2 pcs chili pepper

  • salt and pepper, to taste
  1. In a large cooking pot, add the pork face, pork belly, soy sauce, salt and pepper, and water.

  2. Let it boil and simmer for 40 to 60 minutes or until pork is tender. Remove scum as it rises.

  3. Transfer the ingredients and add the butter, and chopped onions, into a grilling pan.

  4. Grill for 5 to 10 minutes or until the pork is crispy enough on all sides.

  5. Transfer to another container and let it cool for a bit.

  6. Once ready, chop the pork in small fine sizes.

  7. In a large bowl, add the chopped meat, chili peppers, and calamansi. Season with soy sauce and pepper as needed.

  8. Stir the ingredients until well-incorporated.

  9. Transfer to a sizzling hot plate or any other serving plate.

  10. Serve and enjoy!
